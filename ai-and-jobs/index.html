Generative AI and Labor Demand: Evidence from Online Job Markets

Georgi Demirev · interactive companion to the working paper

Online job postings for occupations most exposed to generative AI have fallen sharply relative to the least exposed since the release of ChatGPT (November 2022). This page lets you explore the estimates behind the paper — across five independent measures of occupational AI exposure, two data sources (EU online job adverts and Australia's Internet Vacancy Index), and every individual occupation in the sample.

Headline result: exposure predicts the decline

The main specification regresses the change in log job postings (average of post-ChatGPT quarters over the four quarters preceding the release) on occupational AI exposure, with country fixed effects and standard errors clustered by country. Exposure is scaled 0–1, so each coefficient is the estimated log-point difference between the most and least exposed occupations.

Automation vs augmentation

Two of the exposure measures decompose into an automation component (AI substitutes for the task) and an augmentation component (AI assists a human doing it). Entered jointly, automation exposure predicts fewer postings and augmentation exposure predicts more in the Australian data — the pattern a substitution story implies. On EU data the split is clean only for the Anthropic measure.

Who is hit: entry level and the most senior

Using EU vacancies broken down by required experience (EURES), the association between AI exposure and posting growth follows an inverted U: most negative for entry-level postings and for those requiring over ten years of experience.

Methods & data

Specifications

The long-difference (“delta”) model, estimated once per exposure measure:

Δ log ȳc,i = δ0 + ξc + δ·XAIi + εc,i

where ȳ is the average number of postings in the pre / post period for occupation i in country (or Australian state) c, ξc are country fixed effects, and XAI is exposure scaled to 0–1. The event study:

log yti,c = γt + αc,i + δt·XAIi×𝕀(t) + εti,c

with country×occupation and period fixed effects; 2022Q4 is the omitted reference period. Standard errors are clustered by country (and occupation in the event studies). Cells with fewer than 20 postings in either period are excluded.

Data

  • EU: CEDEFOP Skills-OVATE online job advertisements, ISCO 3-digit occupations × 28 countries, quarterly snapshots (each covering a trailing 12-month window); EURES vacancy data for the experience breakdown.
  • Australia: Jobs and Skills Australia, Internet Vacancy Index, ANZSCO 4-digit occupations × 8 states/territories, , mapped to ISCO via the ABS correspondence.
  • Exposure measures: Felten et al. (2018); Webb (2022); Eloundou et al. (2023); the Anthropic Economic Index usage measure of Handa et al. (2025); and the AI-product exposure measure of Demirev (2024). All standardized to 0–1 across occupations.
